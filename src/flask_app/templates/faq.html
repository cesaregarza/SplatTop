Frequently Asked Questions

What is this?

splat.top is a website that collects and displays Top 500 data from the game Splatoon 3. It adds a time dimension to the rankings available on SplatNet 3, and allows you to see how players have progressed over time. Additionally, it provides a centralized location to view all of a player's Top 500 data for each mode, as well as their weapons, peak data, and aliases they've used in the past.

How often is it updated?

My scraper runs every 15 minutes, and will update the database automatically. It runs on the following schedule: XX:06, XX:21, XX:36, XX:51.

How do you get the data?

I use a scraper that I wrote to get the data from SplatNet 3. It's written in Python and is available on pypi as splatnet3_scraper. It's open source.

Why is it so ugly?

I'm a data scientist, not a web developer. I'm not very good at frontend stuff. If you want to help me make it look better, please feel free to open a pull request on GitHub.

What's the weird number in my profile URL?

That's your internal SplatNet 3 ID. It is persistent across name changes, and is used to identify you in the database. It's also used to generate your profile URL. If you change your name, your profile URL will not change.

I think there's an error in the data. What do I do?

You can either open an issue on GitHub, or you can DM me on Twitter. My DMs are open @JoyTheDataNerd. You can also add me on Discord at pyproject.toml (with the period). I'm also in Sendou.ink's Discord server.
